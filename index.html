The 30-second version: You train stats in the gym by spending Energy. The higher your Happy when you train, the more stat you get — a lot more. Your property sets your base Happy (get this up — §3). A happy jump stacks Happy with cheap candy, doubles it with one Ecstasy, and dumps your Energy in that window. Newbie move: do the Lollipop Jump (§7) — ~$80k, barely addictive.
🌱Day one? Here's the order. Start the Lollipop Jump (§7) as soon as you can scrape together ~$80k — it's cheap and worth doing early; you do not need big stats first. Until you can afford it, just train your four stats with the Energy you regenerate (it's free). Your main early goal is reaching level 15, which unlocks travelling/flying — push for that (here's a great guide to levelling there fast ↗). Train free, jump the moment you can afford it, and leave the expensive Xanax/eDvD methods for much later.

1The absolute basics

Three things drive training:

⚡Energy
😊Happy
🏋Gym dots

⚡Energy is your fuel — it regenerates +5 every 15 minutes normally, or +5 every 10 minutes (~720/day) if you're a donator. Your bar is capped at 100, or 150 for donators. Each gym train spends a fixed chunk. A full bar going unspent = wasted regen.

💲Day-one tip: become a donator. A donator pack is only about $5 and raises your Energy maximum from 100 → 150 — that's 50% more training every single session, so it pays for itself almost immediately. Non-donators are stuck at 100 Energy max; donators get 150. Strongly recommended even on your first day.

😊Happy is the yellow bar and your gains multiplier. Its normal max comes from your property (more on that next). Jumping blows past that cap temporarily.

🏋Gym dots rate how good your gym is per stat (0–10). Better gym = more stat per Energy; you unlock better gyms by training a lot.

🧭Where this stuff is in Torn: your Energy and Happy bars sit at the top of the screen, next to the TCT clock — Torn's own server clock, and the one the :15 Happy-resets follow (watch that clock, not your local time). Buy lollipops, Ecstasy and Xanax from the Items Market (City → Items Market). Train at the Gym (left menu → Gym). Points are an in-game currency you buy from other players for cash (you do not need to open donation packs to get them); the Points Building (City → Points Building) is where you buy Points and do the energy refill. Flying abroad and rehab unlock at level 15, on the Travel page.

New player tip: at the start, just train all four stats evenly until you're around 20k total — a balanced base is most useful early. After that you can start specializing into one stat. (The recipes work the same either way; just train whichever stat you're topping up.)

2Why Happy = gains (the one chart that matters)

Gains rise faster and faster with Happy — doubling Happy gives more than double the gain per Energy. Here's the real curve from the game's formula for an example player:

This is why low-happy players gain almost nothing, and why where you start on this curve — your property — is the single biggest decision you make.

3Your property is the #1 lever — get to 5k Happy ASAP

Your base Happy is set entirely by your property, and it does two huge things: it's the Happy you train at all day when not jumping, and it's the floor every jump multiplies (a jump = (base + candy) × 2). Raise your base and you raise everything at once.

How much does base Happy actually matter? A minute-by-minute sim of pure gym training (no items at all) shows a maxed Private Island player gains about roughly 4–5× more stat per week (and far more — 10×+ — when your stats are still low) than a 100-Happy player — for zero items and zero addiction. Your property out-trains most jump strategies just by existing.

💡Why "ASAP"? Because high base Happy compounds with everything else you ever do — every train, every jump, forever. It's the one upgrade that never stops paying.
💸Don't buy one — rent it. A fully-kitted Private Island is ~$1.95B plus ~$352k/day upkeep. For almost everyone that's a money pit and a waste. The smart move is to lease (rent) one.

Renting gets you the exact same Happy for a tiny fraction of the cost:

  1. Lease from another player. You skip the massive purchase price and the cost of buying all the upgrades — you just cover the daily upkeep — and you still get the property's full Happy. A leased, fully-upgraded PI hands you the whole 5,025.
  2. Just ask on the forums (or in your faction). Established players regularly lease newbies a property to help them grow — often a Ranch (~600–1,925 Happy) or even a Private Island — for as little as $1. Post a polite ask in a newbie/help thread; the community does this a lot.
  3. Only buy once you're seriously rich and want it permanent. Until then, rent and put your money into training.
🏝Upgraded-Happy ladder: Ranch ~600–1,925 · Castle 3,475 · Private Island up to 5,025. Get your base Happy as high as you can cheaply — leasing is how. You can also add the airstrip upgrade for a little extra Happy.

4What a "jump" actually is

Four moves, fast, in order:

  1. Stack Happy with cheap candy (each lollipop = +25). Eat a pile to push Happy over your max.
  2. Double it with one Ecstasy — the multiplier that makes a jump worth it.
  3. Dump your Energy training in the gym, immediately, while Happy is sky-high.
  4. Beat the clock. Over-max Happy resets every 15 minutes, so start a jump just after a :15 mark and click fast.
⏱Timing trick: wait until the clock just passes :00/:15/:30/:45, then candy → Ecstasy → train. Full window, no waste.

5The rules you can't break (cooldowns)

🍬Booster cooldown — limits candy

Each candy adds to your booster cooldown (lollipop +30 min). It caps at 24h, so 49 lollipops/day total — one big jump, or split into several small ones.

💊Drug cooldown — limits drugs

While a drug cooldown is running you simply can't take another drug — the game won't let you. Ecstasy ~4h, Xanax ~8h. (Overdose is a separate, random risk each time you take a drug.)

So a full 49-candy jump = once a day. The workaround, micro-jumps, splits those candies into smaller, more frequent jumps so more of your Energy lands at high Happy.

6Which jump should I do?

★ New & broke? → Lollipop Jump, once a day. ~$80k, almost no addiction. Pure upside.

◆ Best bang for buck? → Lollipop Micro 12h (two small jumps a day — waking & bedtime). Great value, manageable addiction.

▲ Online a lot / max gains? → Micro 4h or Xanax jumps — far more output, but watch addiction and your wallet.

☠ Rich and in a hurry? → eDvD jump. Enormous gains, enormous cost, heavy addiction. Not a beginner move.

☠Studying at university? Before you choose a Xanax- or Ecstasy-heavy method, know that the addiction can get you kicked out of your current education course — losing that course's progress (see §8). While you're enrolled, stick to the low-addiction Lollipop jumps.

7Step-by-step recipes

These recipes assume you've sorted your property first (§3) — the higher your base Happy, the more every jump is worth.

🎟About the energy refill (works on every jump below): at the Points Building you can spend 30 Points (≈$1M) to refill your Energy to 150. Do it during a jump — right after you've trained your Energy, while your Happy is still doubled — and you get to train another 150 at that high Happy. It's highly recommended but totally optional — it costs money and only works once per ~24 hours, so skip it if you're low on Points or saving them.
⚡Energy numbers below assume the 150 (donator) cap. Not a donator? Your max is 100 — wherever a recipe says "fill to 150," read it as 100, and the Energy totals (400, 1,000…) shrink to match.
💰Prices here are per cycle — the cost of a single jump. The comparison table in §9 multiplies these into weekly totals, which is why its numbers look much bigger: a daily lollipop fires ~6×/week, and a 4-Xanax cycle runs ~34h so it lands ~5×/week. Prices are live market and move around. (Items the engine doesn't model are simulated at 48 lollipops/cycle, so a recipe's "49" rounds to ~the same cash.)

🍭🍭 Lollipop Micro-jumps

cheap · more output

Same candy budget, split smaller so more of your Energy is boosted (you can only eat 49 lollipops/day either way).

💰 Per cycle: ~$58K (12h, ~24 lolli) · ~$45K (4h, ~8 lolli) — the Ecstasy is the bulk of each

Twice a day (12h) — the sweet spot

  1. On waking: full Energy, wait for a :15, eat ~24 Lollipops + 1 Ecstasy, train.
  2. Before bed (~12h later): repeat with ~24 + 1 Ecstasy.

Every 4h (rapid) — max value

  1. Every ~4h (6×/day): ~8 Lollipops + 1 Ecstasy, train the Energy built up.
  2. Trains nearly all your daily Energy boosted — best $/stat there is.
⚠Catch: 6 Ecstasy/day = lots of addiction (§8). The 12h version is friendlier.
🎟Energy refill (optional): you only get one per ~24h. On the 12h plan, use it on one of your two jumps for +150 at high Happy. On the 4h plan it covers just 1 of 6 jumps, so it barely helps — save your Points.
Level 2 · your first Xanax jump

💊 The 1-Xanax Jump

~$900k · addictive

One Xanax adds 250 Energy, so you train 400 Energy at high Happy instead of 150. The simplest way to jump bigger.

🍭 49 Lollipops💊 1 Xanax💊 1 Ecstasy🎟 30 Points (optional)

💰 Per cycle: ~$920K (almost all of it the Xanax; +≈$1M if you add the optional refill)

  1. Buy the items (~$900,000 total — almost all of it the Xanax).
  2. Let your Energy fill to 150 (its normal max).
  3. Take the 1 Xanax. Your Energy jumps to 400.
  4. Wait about 8 hours for the Xanax drug cooldown to finish. You can't take the Ecstasy yet — while a drug cooldown is running the game won't let you take another drug.
  5. Once the cooldown is gone, wait until the clock just passes :00, :15, :30, or :45.
  6. Eat all 49 Lollipops.
  7. Take the 1 Ecstasy — your Happy doubles.
  8. Go to the gym and spend all 400 Energy, fast.
  9. (Optional, recommended) Points-refill (+150 Energy, ≈$1M) and spend that 150 too, still in the window.
💡Lollipops, not chocolate. A Big Box of Chocolate gives +35 Happy vs a lollipop's +25 — a tiny edge — but costs ~65× the price, which works out to ~47× worse Happy per dollar. Ecstasy doubles both, so the happy gap stays tiny while the price gap doesn't. Chocolate is a trap.
ℹ️The Xanax's +75 Happy resets at the next :15 mark, so it's gone by jump time — your jump Happy comes from the lollipops + Ecstasy, not the Xanax.

Level 2 · the big one

💊💊💊💊 The 4-Xanax Jump

~$4.4M · very addictive

Bank four Xanax over a day to reach 1,000 Energy, then dump it all in one jump plus a refill. Big gains, but a lot of waiting and addiction.

🍭 49 Lollipops💊 4 Xanax💊 1 Ecstasy🎟 30 Points

💰 Per cycle: ~$4.4M (mostly the 4 Xanax; ~5 of these a week ≈ $22M)

  1. Buy the items (~$4,400,000 total — mostly the 4 Xanax).
  2. First, spend all your current Energy in the gym (train down to 0) — the Energy numbers below assume you start empty.
  3. Take your 1st Xanax. Energy → 250.
  4. Wait about 8 hours (the drug cooldown), then take your 2nd Xanax. Energy → 500.
  5. Wait about 8 hours again, take your 3rd Xanax. Energy → 750.
  6. Wait about 8 hours again, take your 4th Xanax. Energy → 1,000. (Steps 2–5 take roughly a full day.)
  7. Wait about 8 more hours for that last Xanax's cooldown to finish — you can't take the Ecstasy until the cooldown is clear.
  8. Now wait until the clock just passes :00, :15, :30, or :45.
  9. Eat all 49 Lollipops.
  10. Take the 1 Ecstasy — your Happy doubles.
  11. Spend all 1,000 Energy in the gym, fast.
  12. Use a Points energy refill (30 Points, ≈$1M). Energy → 150.
  13. Spend that 150 Energy too — still inside the same 15-minute window.
⚠While Energy is over 150 (the whole day of banking), your normal regen is wasted. And the drugs hit you two separate ways: +160 addiction points (4×35 + 20) toward the brain debuff, and 5 doses (4 Xanax + 1 Ecstasy) toward the separate 150-dose Sports Science Lab lockout (§8) — about 30 such jumps (~6 weeks) would lock you out of that gym.
Level 3 · advanced

📀 The eDvD Jump

~$22M/cycle · very addictive

Use eDvDs (+2,500 Happy each) for absurd Happy. Gains explode; so does the bill.

📀 4–5 eDvD💊 4 Xanax💊 1 Ecstasy🎟 30 Points

💰 Per cycle: ~$21.6M (mostly the 4 eDvD; ~5 a week ≈ $108M — that's the §9 figure)

  1. Bank 4 Xanax over ~a day for ~1,000 Energy.
  2. Wait for cooldowns + a :15. Use 4–5 eDvD (Happy +10–12.5k).
  3. Take Ecstasy — Happy doubles to ~25–35k.
  4. Dump Energy, Points-refill, dump again — all inside one 15-min window.
⚠~5× a beginner's output, but ~$100M/week and the heaviest addiction. Only when time beats money.

8Addiction — the thing that bites newbies

Every drug adds hidden addiction points. This is the real cost of big jumps:

  • Xanax = +35, Ecstasy = +20 per dose.
  • A faction Toleration perk cuts the points each drug adds — at max it roughly halves them (1 Xanax becomes ~18 instead of 35). If your faction has it, you can jump harder for the same addiction.
  • Your body clears about 20/day (~140/week).
  • Too much → growing battle-stat penalty (−1%, −2%, −3%…) shown by a reddening brain icon.

Two different counters, don't confuse them: (1) addiction points — rise with each drug and fall ~20/day; these drive the brain-icon stat debuff and the education kick. (2) lifetime Xanax+Ecstasy doses used — only ever goes up, and once it passes 150 doses it locks you out of the drug-free Sports Science Lab gym.

☠The biggest newbie trap — getting kicked from education. Once addiction reaches roughly a 6–7% brain debuff (with a maxed faction Toleration perk — and sooner, ~5%, without it), you're booted from the education course you're currently enrolled in — just that one course, but you start it over, and a longer course can be weeks of lost progress. Annoying enough to plan around. Watch the brain icon and rehab before it turns red, or stay on low-addiction methods while studying.
⚠Also: once your total Xanax + Ecstasy used passes 150, you lose access to the drug-free Sports Science Lab gym. Rapid-Ecstasy and Xanax jumps blow past that fast — though honestly the Lab's dots are unremarkable, so it's not a gym most people miss.

✅The Lollipop Jump (1 Ecstasy/day) stays addiction-neutral — your daily clear-rate keeps up, so it's the perfect forever-method. Go heavier and you'll need to rehab (~$250k a trip) — fly to Switzerland from the Travel page (unlocks at level 15).

Per-cycle price is for orientation — what you actually track is weekly value

The per-jump prices in §7 tell you what one cycle costs to pull off. But leveling is a long game, and the number that really decides how fast you grow for your money is defense bought per dollar — and that's where the methods split hard. The twist: it's not "training harder" that drains your wallet. The cheapest method per stat, Micro-4h, actually trains nearly all your energy at doubled Happy. The bill explodes only when you start buying energy (Xanax) and buying Happy (eDvD, Mistletoe) — premium consumables, and because the Happy curve (§2) keeps steepening, every extra point of Happy costs more than the last.

Extra defense (above the no-items baseline) bought per $1M spent — one week, same example player. Longer bar = better value. A dollar spent on lollipop jumps buys ~10–21× more defense than the same dollar on eDvD or Mistletoe.

So the splurge methods aren't buying value — they're buying speed: more stat per week, because you can cram a war's worth of training into a few days. If money is your limit (it usually is), steady cheap lollipop jumps win going away over any long stretch — and because gains compound as your stats rise, the value gap only widens the longer you play. Save the eDvD/Mistletoe blowouts for when time is the constraint — a war, a deadline — not as your daily driver.
